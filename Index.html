1. The highest mountain in the world in nature is Mauna Kea, not Mount Everest



We all know that in school we learned that the tallest mountain in the world is Mount Everest, but that's a lie, the tallest mountain in the world is Mauna Kea Mountain situated in Hawaii with an altitude below the surface of 5,998 meters and 4,205 meters above the surface with a total of 10,203 meters.

So tell your teachers they are wrong :)


2. Glass is neither a solid nor a liquid



Do you know that glass is neither a solid nor a liquid? The basic component of glass is silica (SiO2), which is mixed with other components, such as soda and limestone, at temperatures around 1700Â°C. This process results in a viscous, yellowish liquid mixture that resembles honey. On cooling, the mixture hardens, becoming a solid.

According to it's molecular structure, glass is classified neither as a liquid nor as a solid, but as a non-crystalline solid or simply an amorphous solid. Solid crystals are those that show a pattern of symmetry in their molecular composition. As glass does not form this pattern in its structure, it is understood to be non-crystalline or amorphous.


3. African buffalo make decisions by voting



Apparently, democracy is not limited to humanity. For example, African buffalo are known to select which direction they will continue to vote. One by one, the adult females in the herd get up, look in a specific direction and then lie down. The direction that attracts the most attention is often where the herd travels. If the vote is heavily divided, the herd will usually split temporarily.


4. Saturn could float on water



Size has nothing to do with the mass of an object. A giant iceberg, for example. It's less dense than the one underneath, so it can stay on top of the water. Gas also has a lower density than liquid, including water. So, although Saturn is the second largest planet in our solar system, because it is composed almost entirely of gas and ice, the planet could actually float on water.


5. Humans only eat 200 species of plants



Scientists estimate that there are around 400,000 different species of plants on Earth. It's much harder to estimate how many of them are edible, but the numbers range from 80,000 to 300,000. But, in all of the world's cultures combined, we only eat 200 of these plants. Surprisingly, about 50% of the calories from the plants we eat come from just three species: rice, wheat and corn.


6. Planets have sound



Although sound cannot travel through the vacuum of space, NASA has launched several probes that have flown close to planets in our solar systems to make recordings.

It turns out that charged particles in the planets' atmosphere interact to create radio waves, and NASA has translated these waves into sonorous sounds. Each planet has its own unique "music" and it's a bit strange. Jupiter sounds a bit underwater, Neptune sounds like ocean waves and Saturn sounds like the background of a horror movie.


7. "World's most expensive asteroid!"



In 2023, NASA will send an unmanned mission to the asteroid Psyche, an asteroid that could be valued at 10 billion of trillion dollars (this number is written with a 10 followed by 24 zeros). This is because the asteroid Psyche is almost entirely made of metal. It's composition includes iron, nickel and possibly even gold, platinum and cobalt.

Psyche is about 210 kilometers in diameter and is located in the asteroid belt of the solar system. Scientists believe that Psyche could be the core of a planet that will have fragmented over a billion years due to violent collisions.


8. The Moon was once, part of the Earth



The most widely accepted theory about the origin of the Moon is exactly this: When Earth was still a young planet, an object at least the size of Mars collided with it. With that, a piece of the planet broke away, but it didn't pull away completely.

Millions of years later, this piece is known to us as the Moon. This novelty can be a great tool for the poetic and fictional construction of the Universe.

10. Clouds weigh tons!



There are few things more beautiful than the fluffy white clouds that float in a blue sky.

We tend to think they are as light as cotton.

However, clouds are really heavy. The average of a cloud formation (formed by water droplets) is about five hundred tons. But how does it float with all that weight? Well, she only manages that because the atmosphere around her is also very heavy.
